Exercise 1

Goals

To understand and apply the design process from problem identification to prototyping a solution.

Methods Used

Used brainstorming, persona creation, empathy maps, and sketching to analyze the problem and generate ideas.

Key Findings

Discovered that users prioritize simplicity and quick navigation in daily-use applications; emotional connection also plays a big role in retention.

Personal Contributions

I led the creation of our persona and empathy map, and sketched two wireframes based on user needs.

Project 1

Goals

Redesign a public transportation app to improve usability and accessibility for visually impaired users.

Methods Used

Conducted user interviews, accessibility testing, competitive analysis, and iterated using Figma mockups.

Key Findings

Visual clutter and lack of voice-guided feedback were the top issues faced by our participants. Color contrast and text scaling were also critical for usability.

Personal Contributions

I coded the prototype using HTML and CSS, integrated feedback from our testing into a new layout, and ensured accessibility standards (WCAG 2.1) were met.

Project 2

Goals

Design a mobile app that helps college students manage their mental health and daily wellness habits.

Methods Used

Performed secondary research, conducted surveys and interviews, developed wireframes in Figma, and built an interactive prototype with accessibility considerations.

Key Findings

Students appreciated features that offered flexibility and anonymity, especially around journaling and mood tracking. Notifications were most effective when customizable.

Personal Contributions

I analyzed survey results, facilitated interviews, created low- and high-fidelity wireframes, and managed the app’s feature prioritization process.
